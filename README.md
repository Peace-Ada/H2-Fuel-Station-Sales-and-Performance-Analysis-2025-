# 🚀 H₂ Fuel Station Sales & Performance Analysis (2025)

Every day, thousands of vehicles arrive at H₂ Fuel Station to refuel. Behind each transaction lies a story of **customer behavior, product performance, and operational efficiency**. This analysis consolidates a full year of 2025 transaction data to uncover patterns that can help improve **revenue, service delivery, and profitability**.

---

## 📌 Problem Statement
The station management wants to understand:  
- How much revenue is generated monthly and annually  
- Which fuel types drive the most revenue  
- Which customer segments contribute most to sales  
- Peak sales hours and high-demand periods  
- Attendance and pump performance  
- Profitability and areas for operational improvement  

---

## 🧹 Data Cleaning & Preparation
To ensure reliable insights:  
- **12 monthly datasets (Jan–Dec 2025)** were appended into a single consolidated dataset  
- Column names and formats were standardized for **clarity and consistency**  
- Data was checked for **duplicates and missing values** — none were found  
- Calculated new columns for **total revenue, profit margins, and sales metrics**  
- Analysis was conducted using **Excel and Power Query**, ensuring **accuracy and reproducibility**  

---

## 📊 Key Insights
- **Monthly Revenue:** December recorded the highest sales, driven by festive travel and increased transportation demand  
- **Fuel Type Performance:**  
  - **PMS:** Main revenue source, contibuting 59.04% of total revenue  
  - **AGO:** 37.46% of total revenue  
  - **DPK:** 3.50%, with a seasonal spike from March to May  
- - **Peak Hours:** Sales increased between **3 p.m. – 5 p.m.**, which is expected as it aligns with typical closing hours.
  -  This pattern occurs because **trucks are the main customer segment**.
  -  
- **Pump and Attendance Performance:** Pump 4 consistently generated lower revenue; some attendants performed lower than others
-   
- **Profitability:** Overall **profit margin of 3.68%**, within the typical industry range but **room for improvement**

## 💡 Recommendations

1. **Prepare for Peak Periods**  
   - Anticipate higher fuel demand in December and during 3–5 p.m. peak hours.  
   - Ensure **PMS availability**, staffing, and inventory management to prevent queues and lost sales.  

2. **Vehicle Strategy**  
   - **Trucks (main revenue driver):** provide ample fuel, faster refueling lanes, and loyalty programs.  
   - **Other vehicles:** optimize service speed, targeted promotions, and scheduling using buying patterns.  
   - Coordinate attendants, payment processing, and inventory for all vehicles, especially high-volume commercial ones.  

3. **Pump & Attendant Performance**  
   - Check **Pump 4** for operational issues.  
   - Monitor **attendant sales distribution and workload** to ensure consistent performance.  

4. **Profit & High-Margin Products**  
   - Focus on truck customers for volume growth.  
   - Promote **high-margin products**: engine oil, car accessories, convenience items.  
   - Monitor pump accuracy and fuel loss to improve operational efficiency.  

5. **DPK (Kerosene) Strategy**  
   - Monitor seasonal patterns before adjusting inventory.  
   - Apply targeted promotions only if consistent demand is observed; otherwise, prioritize PMS and AGO.  

6. **Overall Efficiency & Customer Experience**  
   - Optimize service periods and promotions using buying patterns.  
   - Ensure fast, smooth service for all customers, especially high-volume commercial vehicles.  
   - Maintain operational readiness to maximize revenue potential.

---

## ✅ Conclusion
After analyzing the dataset, one thing became very clear: H₂ Fuel Station is performing at a **normal level**, with a **profit margin of 3.68%**, which is within the typical industry range but **leaves room for improvement**.

The analysis shows **steady sales and consistent customer demand**, but operational and strategic adjustments — such as optimizing staffing, monitoring pump performance, and focusing on high-revenue products could **improve efficiency and profitability**.

This project demonstrates that **behind every financial transaction lies valuable insights**, and when data is analyzed properly, it can **fuel smarter business decisions and guide operational improvements**.
